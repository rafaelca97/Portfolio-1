01 “Show, don’t tell” is one of the fundamental principles I've been learning in my studies of narrative. It has been present in the conception, design, and development of this personal web portfolio project.

What should I show? Very easy, I must show that I am a Full Stack Developer and all my training to be able to make the leap into the professional world.

With this premise, I had to find a way for the user to know from the outset that I am a Full Stack Developer. And that way is by involving them on the website.

How can they be involved? By using canvas.

02 While I was in the process of seeking inspiration, I was playing Cuphead, a video game with a classic 1930s animation style known as 'Rubber Hose Style.' Truth be told, I fell in love with that style and knew I had to include it there. Thanks to my background in filmmaking, I knew that Disney had many films and shorts with that animation style, such as 'Silly Symphonies' and 'Steamboat Willie,' among many others, where I could find the narrative and visual elements to apply to my website.

Among the possibilities offered by canvas and HTML itself, I decided to create this friendly astronaut with a distinctive cursor, so that the user can interact.

Thanks to my training as a UX/UI specialist, I knew where to place the elements in a visually appealing and device-responsive manner for users viewing this webpage on various devices.

03 Once all the information and visual elements were gathered, it was time to translate everything onto the webpage. Thanks to my training as a web developer, I was able to develop backend code mainly focused on the functionality of the canvases, and frontend code using CSS and HTML5 languages.

With patience and by managing frustration (something quite common in our field), I was able to finish this project. I am proud of it, and it teaches me that the path of learning is infinite and full of possibilities.
